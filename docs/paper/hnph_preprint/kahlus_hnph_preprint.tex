\begin{abstract}
Most automated sleep studies ask which stage a person is in now. HNPH asks a harder but narrower question: given an oracle annotation of the current stage, does EEG recorded before an issue time improve the probability assigned to a stable future annotation beyond what persistence, recent stage history, and time of night already predict? A high forecasting score alone cannot answer this question because it may come from overlapping windows, subject identity, acquisition artifacts, or a weak comparator. We present version 0.4 of the Human Neural Predictability Horizon (HNPH), a falsifiable protocol for estimating the longest contiguous set of future lead bands with residual EEG information. The outcome is an \emph{operational sleep-stage annotation conditioned on oracle current state}, not a biological transition detector. The planned study uses DOD-H for development and keeps DOD-O sealed for external acquisition/population evaluation, conditional on a fail-closed audit of five individual rater streams, person identities, physical metadata, licenses, and immutable hashes. The protocol prespecifies leave-one-rater-out soft targets, person-first splits, disjoint physical-time support, subject-balanced proper-score inference, best-baseline gates, negative controls, and powered simultaneous confidence bounds. Sleep-EDF and CAP are limited to descriptive single-label transport checks. This is a protocol/theory paper: source qualification is unverified, every empirical stage is unrun, and no HNPH frontier is claimed.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and motivation}

Sleep classification and sleep forecasting are different tasks. A classifier estimates the present stage from a present window. A forecaster must use only information available before an issue time to estimate a later outcome. HNPH makes that boundary explicit: after current scored state, recent state history, bout age, elapsed night time, and declared acquisition nuisance are accounted for, does strictly causal EEG history improve a probability distribution for a future stable sleep-stage annotation? Figure~\ref{fig:task} shows the operational task.

The distinction matters because sleep is persistent. A model can look impressive by predicting that the current stage will continue, or by seeing nearly the same samples in both its input and target. HNPH therefore supplies the issue-time state as an oracle annotation and asks only whether EEG adds residual information beyond a strong nuisance model. Even a positive result would concern the specified annotation protocol; it would not detect an unobserved biological transition or establish deployment performance.

This restriction is also an explicit correction to the previous Kahlus draft. Its 127-sample target overlapped the input by 126 samples, and the reported value came from a GRU translator rather than the proposed Neural Field Compiler. The often-quoted 3.116 MSE is therefore retained only as an invalidated historical sidecar result. It is not HNPH evidence, and no plot or metric from that experiment is reused here.

Sleep-stage prediction and forecasting are not new. U-Sleep demonstrated cross-cohort present-stage classification at scale \citep{perslev2021usleep}; SSF-SET forecasts future sleep-stage sequences from past EEG \citep{kweon2026ssf}. HNPH's proposed contribution is instead the nuisance-standardized estimand and evidence contract: a strong state-duration comparator, person-balanced inference, repeated-rater construct audit, an untouched external cohort, and controls evaluated under one gate. A positive, null, blocked, or invalid study must remain distinguishable.

Architectural names, geometry-aware operators, future hardware, disease applications, and passive-PCI hypotheses are outside this protocol. They require later, separately frozen protocols. Version 0.4 evaluates a causal probabilistic EEG forecaster, not NFC and not a clinical system.

\section{Operational target and estimand}

For person $s$ and issue time $t$, let $H_t$ be EEG supported strictly on $[t-L,t)$ and let $Z_t$ contain only declared causal nuisance variables: oracle current macrostate, two preceding states, current bout age, elapsed recording time, recent transition count, and signal-quality features. Subject, site, path, session, and future-label identifiers are forbidden model inputs. Wake, NREM, REM, and unknown annotations are evaluated on the native 30-second grid. A destination transition is stable after two consecutive destination epochs.

For lead band $B$, the five-way outcome is
\[
O_t^B\in\{\text{no event},\text{Wake},\text{NREM},\text{REM},\text{Ambiguous}\}.
\]
The preregistered bands are $(0.5,2]$, $(2,5]$, $(5,10]$, and $(10,20]$ minutes; $(2,5]$ is primary. Unknown intervals and rater disagreement are scored as Ambiguous rather than silently discarded. Incomplete follow-up is excluded and counted by band.

Let $q_b(o\mid Z_t)$ be the best eligible nuisance distribution selected on development data and $q_\theta(o\mid H_t,Z_t)$ an EEG-augmented distribution. The subject-level gain is
\begin{equation}
G_s(B)=\frac{1}{|\mathcal A_s|}\sum_{t\in\mathcal A_s}
\log_2\frac{q_\theta(O_t^B\mid H_t,Z_t)}{q_b(O_t^B\mid Z_t)},
\label{eq:gain}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal A_s$ contains complete natural-grid anchors. The population estimand $\Delta(B)=\E_s[G_s(B)]$ therefore gives equal weight to people, not windows. Log score is strictly proper and evaluates the full predictive distribution \citep{gneiting2007proper}. The empirical frontier is the longest contiguous lead-band prefix for which the simultaneous one-sided 95\% subject-cluster lower bound exceeds the preregistered 0.02-bit design threshold and every calibration/control gate passes. An isolated later band cannot skip a failed earlier band.

\section{Fail-closed source and label qualification}

The Dreem Open Datasets publication describes DOD-H and DOD-O as openly distributed polysomnography cohorts independently scored by five sleep technologists \citep{guillot2020dreem}. The project repository and versioned archive are treated only as candidate official sources until locally hash-qualified \citep{dreemrepo,dodzenodo}. Protocol v0.4 assigns DOD-H to development and DOD-O to sealed external acquisition/population evaluation. That assignment is conditional, not an assertion about the contents of any local download.

Before migration, a local qualification packet must verify dataset/version, license, immutable archive hash, stable person identity, channel-level physical units and sampling rates, and five separately identifiable annotation streams with per-rater hashes. If individual labels are absent, the migration stops and HNPH remains protocol-only. Consensus labels cannot replace the repeated-rater gate. Sleep-EDF and CAP may support explicitly descriptive transport plots, but their single-label role under this protocol cannot enable construct-validity claims \citep{sleepedf2018,capsleep2012}.

For each target rater $r$, the soft target is computed only from other raters; $r$ must not appear among its consensus contributors. At least three independent contributing raters are required at every scored target. The target artifact binds target-rater identity, contributing-rater identities, annotation hashes, stage mapping, and target hash. Figure~\ref{fig:contract} summarizes the full leakage boundary.

\section{Study design and decision gates}

People are split before anchors, transforms, calibration, baseline selection, or model fitting. Every input and target interval is represented in physical time and must be disjoint, including filter support and guard intervals. DOD-O remains inaccessible until the protocol, model family, seeds, calibration method, comparators, report template, and source qualification are frozen.

The baseline ladder is empirical no-event/destination frequency, Markov prior, semi-Markov competing risk, validation-tuned logistic or ridge hazard, validation-tuned gradient-boosted hazard, and fixed standard EEG features plus nuisance. Any candidate must beat the best eligible comparator, not merely a convenient weak baseline. The semi-Markov comparator must pass dwell-time fit, held-person calibration and Brier decomposition, synthetic no-EEG dominance, simpler-Markov comparisons, and a flexible nuisance-challenger audit.

Required controls are label shuffle, within-person time shift, unrelated context, missingness-only prediction, applicable channel/geometry permutation, cluster-permutation null, a nuisance probe, a known-signal positive control, and a synthetic null that must fail. Confidence intervals use at least 2,000 person-cluster bootstrap replicates with max-$t$ multiplicity control. Feasibility requires at least 12 independent person clusters, 8 event people, 100 positive primary-band anchors, and frozen simulations indicating at least 80\% power for the 0.02-bit design threshold. These are design criteria, not clinical or biological effect sizes.

Figure~\ref{fig:flow} is the execution order. Every stage is \unrun. Source qualification precedes development; development gates precede model-family freeze; and only then may the sealed external cohort be opened once. A valid null stops architecture expansion. A failed control blocks a scientific interpretation. An integrity failure invalidates the execution rather than becoming a null result.

\section{Conclusion}

HNPH v0.4 is a falsifiable protocol for an oracle-conditioned operational annotation frontier. It neither claims a measured horizon nor mistakes current-state persistence for future information. Its scientific value will come from making a supported frontier, a bounded null, a blocked construct, and an invalid execution separately auditable. At present the honest outcome is blocked source qualification. Figure polish does not change that status.

\section{Proper-score decomposition and assumption-dependent ceiling}

Let $p_1(o)=p(o\mid H,Z)$ and $p_0(o)=p(o\mid Z)$. For oracle distributions,
\begin{equation}
\E\left[\log_2\frac{p(O\mid H,Z)}{p(O\mid Z)}\right]=\I_2(O;H\mid Z).
\label{eq:cmi}
\end{equation}
This identity follows directly from conditional mutual information. For fitted distributions,
\begin{align}
\E\log_2\frac{q_\theta(O\mid H,Z)}{q_b(O\mid Z)}
=\;&\I_2(O;H\mid Z)
-\frac{1}{\ln 2}\E\KL(p_1\Vert q_\theta) \\
&+\frac{1}{\ln 2}\E\KL(p_0\Vert q_b).
\label{eq:decomposition}
\end{align}
Positive fitted gain can therefore reflect baseline misspecification, which is why the comparator ladder is part of the scientific gate.

A nontrivial universal finite horizon does not follow from noisy noninvasive observation alone. If an explicitly specified latent process $U_t$ generates observed EEG and future annotations through an appropriate conditional channel, data processing may yield
\[
\I(O_{t+h};H_t\mid Z_t)\leq \I(U_{t+h};U_{\leq t}\mid Z_t).
\]
Additional mixing or strong data-processing assumptions can make the right-hand side contract with $h$ \citep{cover2006elements}. No contraction constants are estimated here. Figure~\ref{fig:theory} therefore labels the ceiling as a standard, assumption-dependent consequence and not an empirical or novel contribution.
